Where should your next dollar go?

Retirement accounts sound complicated. They are not. Every account answers one question: when does the tax hit? There are only three answers. This site teaches you all three, then shows you exactly what to do.

The short version

If you only remember five things

This is the order most people should send their money. The full guide explains every step. But even alone, this list puts you ahead of most of the country.

Grab the matchPut enough in your 401(k) to get every dollar your employer matches. It is free money. Nothing else comes close.
Kill expensive debtPay off anything charging over 7%, like credit cards. Paying it off is a guaranteed win.
Fill the HSAIf your health plan qualifies, max it and invest it. It is the only account that is never taxed.
Fill a Roth IRAUp to $7,500 a year. Pay tax once, then it grows tax-free forever. High earner? There is a legal side door.
Finish the 401(k)Work toward the full $24,500. Anything beyond that goes into a regular investing account.

That is the skeleton. The guide adds the numbers, the traps, and what to do at your exact income and age.
